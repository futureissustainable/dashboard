## 1. DIAGNOSIS — Where We Are and What's Broken

**The account:** @biobuilds. ~6,500 followers. 62 posts in the last year.

**The numbers that matter:**
- 62 posts produced 3,283 follows. That's 52 follows per post average. Not bad for a construction company. Terrible for a brand that wants to own the category.
- 17,980 total likes across a full year. Some meme pages get that on a Tuesday.
- Average reach per post: 9,877. That's roughly 150% of follower count, which actually means the content *is* reaching non-followers. Instagram is pushing it. Just not far enough.
- 93 saves per post average. This is unusually high. People are bookmarking these posts like a shopping wishlist. Strong purchase intent signal. The content converts interest — it just doesn't generate enough of it.

**What's actually broken:**

The content oscillates between two modes and neither is calibrated for growth:

Mode 1 — Beautiful house photo + short poetic caption. This works. Posts #1, #5, #6, #10 all follow this pattern. But it caps out at ~1,300 likes because there's no share trigger. People admire it. They save it. They don't send it to anyone.

Mode 2 — Product info dump with specs. This tanks. Every time. The audience didn't come here to read about U-values. They came to feel something.

The missing mode — and the one that actually goes viral on Instagram in 2026 — is content engineered to make someone grab their phone and DM it to their partner, their friend, their parents. Content that creates an emotional gap so strong the viewer *needs* someone else to see it.

**Other broken things:**
- Zero carousels posted. In a year. Carousels are the highest-engagement format on Instagram right now. For a visual product like houses, this is leaving growth on the table.
- Reels underperform photos by 4x on follower conversion (18 follows/reel vs 71 follows/image). Not because Reels are bad — because the Reels we made were bad. Configurator walkthroughs, expo invites, product demos. Wrong content, wrong length, wrong hook.
- 25 posts in December (daily posting) suppressed the algorithm. Engagement per post cratered. Instagram read it as spam behavior.
- Every post uses 8-12 hashtags. Hashtags are dead. Instagram killed hashtag following in Dec 2024. Mosseri said publicly they don't help. Posts without hashtags now reach 23% more people.
- All captions in English. The highest-value audience (DACH market: Germany, Austria, Switzerland) speaks German. That's a friction point on every single post.

**What's NOT broken:**
- The photography is genuinely world-class. Winter shots with warm interior glow are a visual signature that nobody else in the modular housing space has.
- The save rate proves the audience has purchase intent. These aren't casual scrollers. They're future buyers building a mental shortlist.
- Posts with contrast hooks ("€2,000 vs €100", "-10°C outside, 24°C inside", "3 weeks vs 2 years") consistently outperform everything else. The formula exists. We just haven't systematized it.

## 2. PLATFORM CONTEXT — What Instagram Actually Rewards Right Now

Forget everything from 2023. The algorithm has fundamentally changed.

**Three signals decide if your post lives or dies:**

**Signal 1: Watch time.** How long people look at your post. For images and carousels, it's dwell time. For Reels, it's completion rate and rewatches. A 7-second Reel watched twice beats a 60-second Reel where everyone leaves at second 5. Instagram measures this as a ratio — percentage watched, not total seconds.

**Signal 2: Sends (DM shares).** This is the big one. Mosseri confirmed sends are weighted 3-5x higher than likes for reaching new audiences. When someone DMs your post to a friend, Instagram reads that as the strongest possible endorsement. 694,000 Reels are sent via DM every minute. This is the single most important metric for growth in 2026.

**Signal 3: Likes-per-reach.** Not total likes. The ratio. 100 likes from 1,000 people reached (10%) beats 500 likes from 10,000 reached (5%). This means a tight, engaged audience is better than a loose, massive one.

**How distribution works now:**
Instagram tests your Reel with a small pool of random non-followers first. If they engage (watch, like, send), it gets pushed to more non-followers. Then to your followers. Then to Explore. The content has to prove itself with strangers before it reaches your own audience.

**What gets suppressed:**
- Reposted content (Instagram uses visual fingerprinting — 70%+ similarity = flagged)
- TikTok watermarks (instant reach penalty)
- Hashtag spam (anything over 5 specific hashtags is noise)
- Daily posting over extended periods (triggers spam classification)
- AI-generated imagery that reads as "too perfect" (Mosseri's year-end memo: Instagram will prioritize raw human content over synthetic)

**What gets boosted:**
- Original content (Instagram increased original content in recommendations to 75% in Q4 2025)
- Content made with Instagram's Edits app (confirmed slight boost by Mosseri)
- Carousel posts (Instagram re-shows unseen slides to people who scrolled past — free second impressions)
- Content that generates saves + sends together
- Collaborative posts (3.4x higher engagement than solo posts)

## 3. AUDIENCE SEGMENTS — Who We're Actually Trying to Reach

We don't want vanity metrics from demographics that will never buy. A viral post that reaches 2 million retired Romanians on Facebook is worthless. We need the right 100,000 people, not the wrong 10 million.

### DACH (Primary market)
**Who:** People building or planning to build homes in Germany, Austria, Switzerland. Don't over-specify demographics.
**Psychology:** They've been researching. They distrust marketing. They respond to proof, not promises. They compare obsessively. They save posts to show their partner later.
**What makes them send a DM to their partner:** A contrast so sharp it makes traditional construction look insane. A visual so warm it makes them feel the life they want. A number so specific it makes them stop scrolling.
**Content trigger:** "Send this to your partner and don't say anything."

### Romania (Primary market)
**Who:** Young families, 30-45. Roughly 70% work in IT, the rest are entrepreneurs or in higher-paying professions. NOT luxury buyers. Practical, fed up with traditional construction.
**Psychology:** They've seen traditional Romanian construction fail (delays, cost overruns, poor insulation). They want out of that system. They respond to speed, cost breakdowns, and proof that this actually works.
**What makes them engage:** Horror stories about traditional construction they recognize. Speed comparisons. Specific cost breakdowns.
**Content trigger:** A contrast so specific it makes the old way look absurd.

### Rest of Europe (Secondary reach)
**Who:** Anyone else thinking about building. Don't box this in. Good data and sharp visuals do the work.
**What makes them share:** Technical details presented beautifully. Surprise that modular can be Passivhaus certified. Numbers that challenge assumptions.

### Who we do NOT want:
- Romanian boomers engaging with emotional fluff who will never afford the product
- "Tiny home" enthusiasts who want a €15K container and think everything else is overpriced
- DIY builders who want to replicate rather than buy
- Engagement farmers, bots, giveaway chasers

Every piece of content should be filtered: **does this attract Segment A, B, or C? If no, don't post it.**

## 4. VOICE & TONE — How to Write, What to Avoid, What to Test

### The BIOBUILDS voice on Instagram

Not a corporation. Not a influencer. Not a tech startup.

BIOBUILDS writes like **a quietly confident architect who just built something that makes every other house look like a compromise.** They don't brag. They state facts that are so impressive they sound like bragging. They let the contrast do the talking.

### Rules:

**Write like you're texting a smart friend, not presenting to a boardroom.**
- Yes: "Same winter. Different home."
- No: "Experience innovation, sustainability, and wellness—with zero compromises."

**Every caption must pass the "send test."** Would one partner screenshot this and send it to the other? If the answer is "they'd just like it and keep scrolling," the caption needs rewriting.

**Lead with the contrast, not the product.**
- Yes: "The average European will spend €2,000 on heating this year. BIOBUILDS homeowners will spend less than €100."
- No: "BIOBUILDS homes are Passivhaus certified, featuring energy demand of 15 kWh/m²/year."

Both say the same thing. The first one makes you feel something. The second one makes you feel like you're reading a brochure.

**Short punchy or meaningful long. No middle ground.** Either go ultra-short (under 50 words, the caption IS the hook) or go long enough to tell a real story (200-400 words with a genuine narrative arc). The worst performing captions are medium-length product descriptions. They're too long to be punchy and too short to be compelling.

**Never use these words:** Revolutionary. Cutting-edge. Game-changer. Seamless. Elevate. Unlock. Journey. Excited to announce. Innovation (unless in a sentence that actually describes the specific innovation).

**Always use these patterns:**
- "X vs Y" contrast ("3 weeks vs 3 years", "€100 vs €2,000", "-10° vs 24°")
- Questions that create a mental gap ("You pay for your house twice. Did you know that?")
- Statements so specific they feel like secrets ("The walls store more carbon than they emit. Every home is a net subtraction from the atmosphere.")

### Bilingual strategy:
- DACH audience gets German captions with English in on-screen text/overlays
- Romanian audience gets Romanian captions
- International/English captions for architecture and design community reach
- Test: alternate languages week by week and track which pulls more from which segment. No assumptions. Data.

### Things to test on voice:
- First person from Alin vs third person brand voice (does the founder's perspective outperform?)
- Question hooks vs statement hooks vs number hooks (track sends per type)
- Caption length: ultra-short (<30 words) vs story-length (200+ words) — kill the medium zone and see what happens
- Provocative vs inspirational tone ("Traditional construction is a scam" vs "There's a better way to build")

## 5. VISUAL GUIDELINES — What Actually Works

### The proven visual formula (from our own data):

The top-performing BIOBUILDS posts share a specific visual DNA:

**Winter + warm interior glow + nature + human presence.**

Posts #1, #5, #7, #10, #11, #12, #14, #25 all follow this. It's the visual signature. It works because it creates an instant emotional contrast — the cold outside vs the warmth inside — which is literally the metaphor for the entire brand.

### Visual do's:

**Shoot in golden hour or blue hour.** The warm light through windows at dusk is the single strongest visual trigger in the dataset. Schedule shoots around it.

**Include human presence.** A kid on the deck. A couple through the window. Bare feet on the wood floor. The house with people in it outperforms the house without people every time. People don't want to buy a house. They want to buy a life.

**Show the contrast physically.** Snow on the railing, t-shirts inside. Rain on the glass, dry warmth inside. Morning mist in the valley, coffee steam on the deck.

**Imperfect is better than perfect.** The 2026 algorithm rewards raw content. Phone-shot Stories of real families in real BIOBUILDS homes. Slightly messy living rooms. A dog on the couch. Real life > rendered perfection. Instagram is actively downranking content that looks AI-generated.

**Use the surrounding landscape aggressively.** The house in the forest clearing. The house on the hill above the valley. The house in the German countryside. The context sells the dream more than the product.

### Visual don'ts:

**Never post wrapped modules on a truck.** Posts #59, #61, #62 are consistently the worst performers. The audience doesn't care about logistics. They care about the life inside.

**Never post empty interiors with no context.** An empty room with no human trace feels like a showroom, not a home. Add a book on the table, a blanket on the couch, a cup of coffee. Stage the life, not the product.

**Never post expo booth photos.** Conference content dies on Instagram. Every time. Route that to LinkedIn or Stories only.

**Never post renders when real photos exist.** Real beats rendered. Always.

**Stop posting the same 142m² exterior angle.** Variety in framing matters. Drone, ground level, through-the-window, detail shots of materials, macro of the wood grain. The house is the same. The perspective must change.

### Carousel visual structure (NEW — never been tested):

Slide 1: The hook image. The exterior at its most dramatic. Text overlay with the contrast statement.
Slide 2-3: Approach. Getting closer. The deck, the entrance, the surroundings.
Slide 4-6: Inside. Room by room. Warm, lived-in, human.
Slide 7: The detail. A material close-up. Wood grain. Light through triple-glazed glass. Something tactile.
Slide 8-10: The life. Family moment. Morning coffee. Evening on the deck. This is the emotional payoff.

No logo on every slide. No spec sheet slides. No "swipe for more info." Let the images carry the story.

### Things to test on visuals:
- Carousel posts vs single images (same photo set — does splitting it into carousel outperform the best single shot?)
- Phone-shot "real life" content vs professional photography (do raw phone shots from actual owners outperform?)
- Drone footage openings on Reels vs ground-level openings
- Text overlays vs clean images with caption-only copy
- Vertical (4:5) vs square framing on feed posts (4:5 takes 20% more screen space)

## 7. POSTING MECHANICS — Timing, Length, Format, Technical Specs

### Posting frequency:
**3 posts per week on @biobuilds. Maximum.**
- 1 carousel (lifestyle walkthrough or contrast story)
- 1 static image (the proven formula — winter/warm/nature/human)
- 1 short Reel (7-15 seconds, visual hook, no talking head)
- Stories: daily or near-daily. Behind the scenes, polls, Q&A, real owner content.

**Why not more:** Your December daily posting experiment proved the algorithm suppresses volume. SocialPilot's analysis of 4.4 million posts confirms: whether posting 5 or 15 times weekly, reach stays flat. Quality and spacing > volume.

### Optimal posting times (to test):
- DACH audience: 7-8 AM CET (morning scroll), 12-1 PM CET (lunch), 7-9 PM CET (evening)
- Romanian audience: Similar windows, same timezone
- Start with Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday and rotate. Track reach by day. No assumptions.

### Format specs:
- **Static images:** 1080×1350px (4:5 vertical). Takes maximum screen real estate. Never square. Never horizontal.
- **Carousels:** Same 4:5 ratio. 8-10 slides optimal. Can mix photos and video clips. Adding audio/music makes carousels eligible for the Reels feed (free extra distribution).
- **Reels:** 1080×1920px (9:16 vertical). 7-15 seconds for virality. 30-60 seconds ONLY for story-driven content. Hook in first 1.5 seconds. Captions/subtitles always on (most people watch on mute).
- **Stories:** 1080×1920px. Use polls, questions, sliders. Engagement on Stories feeds the algorithm's assessment of your account health.

### Caption structure:
- **First 125 characters are everything.** This is what shows before "...more." The hook lives here. The contrast. The number. The question. If this doesn't stop the scroll, nothing below it matters.
- **Keywords over hashtags.** Write "Passivhaus certified modular home" naturally in the caption instead of #passivhaus #modularhome. Instagram's search engine reads captions now.
- **If using hashtags at all:** Maximum 3-5. Hyper-specific only. #PassivhausCertified > #architecture. #ModularHomeEurope > #modernhome. But test zero hashtags against 3-5 and track reach difference.

### Trial Reels:
Use them. Every week. They show content ONLY to non-followers. Zero risk. Fast data. One creator got 2.3M impressions from 9 Trial Reels in 14 days. Test hooks, formats, and tones you're not sure about. Let the data decide before committing to the main feed.

### Collaboration posts:
Use the collab feature with every owner who agrees. Co-authored posts get 3.4x more engagement. When a BIOBUILDS owner posts their home, collab it to @biobuilds. Both audiences see it. Both accounts grow.

### DO:

- ✅ Engineer every post to be DM-sent between partners
- ✅ Post carousels. You've never tried them. The data says they win.
- ✅ Lead with contrast hooks: old world vs BIOBUILDS world
- ✅ Show people living in the houses, not empty rooms

- ✅ Write bilingual content for DACH market

- ✅ Space posts 2-3 days apart minimum
- ✅ Make the founder the face. People follow people.

### DON'T:

- ❌ Post wrapped modules on trucks. Ever. Bottom performers, every time.
- ❌ Use more than 5 hashtags. Or better yet, test zero.
- ❌ Post daily for extended periods (December proved this kills reach)
- ❌ Write medium-length product-description captions. Go short-punchy or long-story. No middle.
- ❌ Post configurator demos or screen recordings as Reels
- ❌ Use words like "revolutionary," "game-changer," "seamless," "elevate"
- ❌ Post expo/conference booth photos to the main feed
- ❌ Cross-post from TikTok with the watermark visible
- ❌ Repost other accounts' content (Instagram penalizes 10+ reposts in 30 days)
- ❌ Post empty interiors with no human presence or life signs
- ❌ Lead with specs. Lead with feeling. Prove with specs.
- ❌ Post testimonial videos to the main feed (high trust, zero engagement — route to paid ads/retargeting)
- ❌ Use the same exterior angle repeatedly. Change the perspective.
- ❌ Write captions that any prefab competitor could also write
- ❌ Assume what works. Test it. Kill what doesn't. Scale what does.

Instagram in 2026 rewards content that doesn't feel like content. The platform is flooded with AI-polished, over-produced, formulaic posts. What cuts through is real. Specific. Human. A kid on a deck in winter. A number that makes traditional construction look insane. A father who built a company because his son couldn't breathe.

BIOBUILDS already has the product, the story, and the visuals. The gap is format, frequency, and share engineering.

Test relentlessly. Post less but better. Make every image answer the question: "Would someone grab their phone and send this to the person they want to build a life with?"

If yes, post it. If no, reshoot it until the answer is yes.
