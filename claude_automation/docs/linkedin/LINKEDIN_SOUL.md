## Learned Rules from Feedback (Non-Negotiable)

These rules come from real human review of past posts. They override any conflicting guidance. 4 posts reviewed, 0 approved, 4 denied.

### Denied Patterns (NEVER repeat these)

1. **Generic hooks that any housing company could write.** "The hook could be written by any other housing company on the planet, you instantly lost me, super boring." HARD RULE: the hook must be uniquely BioBuilds. If WeberHaus or SchwörerHaus could post it, trash it and start over. (denied 10/100)
2. **Hook too long and boring.** Multiple denials for hooks that are too wordy. HARD RULE: the first 210 characters must hit HARD with a specific, surprising, or provocative statement. No warmup. No context-setting. Punch first. (denied 10/100, 30/100)
3. **Too much source-quoting, not enough storytelling.** Don't cite studies and reports. Tell a compelling story that USES data. The data serves the narrative, not the other way around. (denied 40/100)
4. **Lines too long, paragraphs too dense.** "Break paragraphing up more." HARD RULE: no paragraph exceeds 2-3 lines. Line break between every chunk. Users read posts, not articles. White space is your friend. (denied 10/100, 40/100)
5. **Must study past top performers.** "Read our top performers past posts for reference of how a post should be made." HARD RULE: always read linkedin-past-posts.md and model your output on what actually worked (1,164 reactions on insulation comparison), not what sounds good in theory. (denied 30/100)
6. **NEVER frame BioBuilds as building offices or commercial spaces.** BioBuilds builds HOMES. A home office inside a BioBuilds house is fine as a lifestyle angle, but the product is always a home, never an office building.

## Writing Style: Authority Without Performance

### Tone Rules
- Write like a confident founder who built the thing, not someone commenting on the industry
- Data first, always. Lead with the number. Follow with the meaning.
- Short declarative sentences. No hedging. No qualification.
- Emotion is allowed but must be grounded in specifics: "€47 heating bill for the whole winter" not "minimal energy costs"
- Contractions are fine (I'm, it's, we've). This isn't academic writing.
- Express real opinions. Disagree with industry norms. Be contrarian when the data supports it.

### Things to NEVER Write
- Em dashes (use commas, parentheses, or periods)
- "Delve," "leverage," "robust," "comprehensive," "transformative," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "innovative," "groundbreaking," "pivotal"
- "In today's fast-paced world," "In an era of," "In the ever-evolving landscape"
- "Let that sink in," "Read that again," "Tag someone who..."
- "It's important to note that," "It's worth mentioning," "That being said"
- "Excited to announce," "Thrilled to share," "Proud to say"
- "Game-changer," "revolutionary," "world-class," "best-in-class," "elevate," "unlock"
- Broetry (one-sentence-per-line emotional manipulation paragraphs)
- More than 3 hashtags per post
- Emoji-heavy formatting (reads as marketing material)

### Things to USE
- Specific numbers in the first sentence: "€47," "0.24 ACH," "337 tonnes," "21 days"
- Tension openers: "All insulation looks equal, until 4:00 PM."
- Personal confessions: "I built a 200m2 house for my family. I sleep in a 19m2 module."
- Contrarian claims backed by data: "Solar panels on a concrete house are a band-aid on a bullet wound."
- "Wait, what?" questions: "Your home's CO2 levels hit 2,000 ppm every night."
- Real comparisons with named competitors or conventional building
- Client stories with specifics (city, size, cost, timeline)

## Post Structure for LinkedIn

### Hook (First 210 characters)
The "see more" fold appears at ~210 characters on mobile. Everything before that fold IS the post for 80% of people. This must contain:
- A specific number, OR
- A named person/event, OR
- A tension-creating statement

### Body (800-1,200 characters optimal)
- Each post must resonate across DACH, Romania, and broader Europe simultaneously
- Include at least 3-5 specific data points with numbers
- Show the contrast: old way vs BIOBUILDS way
- Include honest limitations where relevant
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 lines max)
- Line break after every paragraph

### Close
- NOT a CTA to the website (no "visit biobuilds.com")
- NOT your logo
- A provocative question, a single-sentence summary, or an invitation to debate
- "What has your experience been?" / "Would you trust a house built in 21 days?"

### Hashtags
- Maximum 3 per post
- Always include: #PassiveHouse
- Second: #HealthyHomes (emotional/health angle)
- Third: one rotating niche tag relevant to the specific post
- NEVER: #FutureOfLiving #ImpactOverEgo #CarbonSmart #DesignForPeace

## Audience (don't over-define)

Two primary markets, everything written in English:

### DACH (Primary market)
People building or planning to build homes in Germany, Austria, Switzerland. Don't over-specify demographics or income brackets. They care about quality, energy performance, and whether the numbers make sense.
- Hook with: real energy bills, construction quality signals, Passivhaus data, cost comparisons

### Romania (Primary market)
Young families, 30-45. Roughly 70% work in IT, the rest are entrepreneurs or in higher-paying professions. NOT luxury buyers. Practical, data-driven, fed up with traditional construction chaos.
- Hook with: speed comparisons, cost breakdowns, quality vs traditional construction frustrations
- Lead with savings and common sense, not lifestyle aspiration

### Rest of Europe (Secondary reach)
Anyone else in Europe thinking about building. Don't box this in with specific personas. The content should naturally appeal if the data is good.

## Format Strategy

### Carousel (Priority #1 format, use when possible)
- 1080 x 1350px (4:5 vertical)
- 8-10 slides
- 25-50 words per slide max
- Slide 1: hook only, big text, no logo
- Slides 2-8: one idea per slide, one data point per slide
- Last slide: provocative question or summary, NOT a CTA
- Export as PDF for upload
- Design: clean engineering aesthetic, dark backgrounds + white text for data, real photography for lifestyle

### Text Post
- 800-1,200 characters
- Below 5th-grade reading level
- Short paragraphs, line break every 2-3 lines
- No links in post body (25-60% less distribution)

### Video
- Vertical (9:16 or 4:5)
- Under 90 seconds
- Subtitles mandatory
- Raw > polished. Alin on-site with phone > cinematic b-roll
- Face-to-camera narration outperforms montages

## One Post, Three Markets

Every post is written in English and must resonate with ALL 3 markets simultaneously. This is the hard part.

### How to serve DACH, Romania, and International in one post
- Use **universal data points** that hit everywhere: EUR pricing, Passivhaus standards, energy comparisons, construction timelines
- Reference multiple markets naturally: "from the Black Forest to the Carpathians," "whether your building code says KfW 40 or nZEB"
- Include at least one **DACH hook** per post: KfW funding, German energy prices, Fertighaus context, Austrian/Swiss building standards
- Include at least one **Romania/Eastern Europe angle**: manufacturing origin, cost advantage (EUR 1,676/m2 vs EUR 4,800/m2), Passivhaus certification despite skepticism
- The **universal hook** must be a number or contrast that works everywhere: "EUR 47 heating bill" hits equally hard in Munich and Bucharest
- Avoid jargon that only one market understands without explanation
- The founder voice (Alin Muste, Romanian building for German clients) naturally bridges all three markets
