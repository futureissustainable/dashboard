## 2. PLATFORM CONTEXT ‚Äî What LinkedIn rewards right now (Feb 2026)

LinkedIn deployed a new AI ranking model called **360Brew** in 2025. It replaced the old engagement-based feed with a relevance-based feed. Three signals matter now. Everything else is noise.

**Signal 1: Dwell time.** How long someone spends on your post. This is THE metric. A text post gets 15-30 seconds. A carousel gets 2-3 minutes (every slide swipe = engagement signal). A video that holds past 8 seconds gets boosted. The algorithm literally trains a model to predict how long each member will spend on each post, then ranks accordingly.

**Signal 2: Comment depth.** Not how many comments. How substantive they are, and whether commenters have relevant job titles. Five architects debating your claim about CLT is worth more than 200 people typing "Great post!" The algorithm measures reply chains. A comment that generates 3 replies is worth 10 standalone comments.

**Signal 3: Topical authority.** The algorithm now scores whether your account consistently posts about the same domain. If Alin posts about passive construction for 3 months straight, then drops a post about passive construction ‚Äî it gets boosted. If he suddenly posts about AI productivity tips, it gets suppressed. Consistency in topic = higher base distribution.

**What's being killed:**

- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree," "Tag someone who...") ‚Äî detected by pattern matching, distribution throttled
- Engagement pods ‚Äî detected with high accuracy, triggering shadowbans
- AI-generated content ‚Äî 30% less reach, 55% lower engagement vs human-written
- Recycled content ‚Äî 84% less reach for identical or near-identical reposts
- Hashtag stuffing ‚Äî 3+ hashtags = 29% less reach
- Self-commenting (posting first comment on your own post) ‚Äî now LIMITS reach, reversed from previous years
- Broetry (one-sentence-per-line emotional manipulation) ‚Äî actively penalized

**What's being rewarded:**

- Carousels: 278% more engagement than video, 596% more than text-only
- Native vertical video under 90 seconds (usage up 69% YoY)
- Posts that generate saves (LinkedIn's new bookmark metric = strong signal)
- Content from accounts with high topical authority scores
- Posts that spark discussion among people with relevant professional backgrounds
- The golden hour: engagement in first 60-90 minutes determines ~70% of total reach

## 3. AUDIENCE SEGMENTS

You don't want 300K likes from random people. You want the right 10,000 eyeballs that either buy a house, invest in your company, or amplify your message to people who do.

**Segment 1: DACH (Primary market)**

LinkedIn language: German posts for organic DACH reach, English for broader distribution. Test both. Track which gets more profile visits from DACH.

**Segment 2: Romania (Primary market)**

What they don't care about: Abstract sustainability arguments. Carbon sequestration numbers. Most Romanian buyers are driven by cost savings and lifestyle upgrade, not environmental ethics. Lead with savings, follow with planet.

**Segment 3: Tourism Entrepreneurs**

Who: People building or expanding glamping sites, boutique resorts, Airbnb portfolios. Romania, Austria, Southern Germany primarily. They think in ROI per unit, occupancy rates, and guest experience differentiation.

What makes them stop scrolling: "This cabin pays for itself in 14 months" with real booking data. Porumbacu Garden as a case study. The relocatability angle (ground screws = you can move the asset). Energy independence = off-grid capability = locations that were previously unbuildable.

What they don't care about: Anything that doesn't connect to revenue generation within the first two sentences.

**Segment 4: Architects & Building Professionals**

Who: Architects, engineers, sustainability consultants across the EU. They influence purchasing decisions. They specify materials and systems. Getting them to recommend BIOBUILDS to their clients is a multiplier.

What makes them stop scrolling: Technical depth. The membrane durability test. The airtightness numbers (0.24 ACH at 50 Pa ‚Äî that's flex-worthy). The EPD data. Comparison tables between building systems. These people respect evidence, not emotion.

What they don't care about: "The future of living" type language. They've heard it from every prefab startup that went bankrupt. Show them the test results and shut up.

**Segment 5: Investors & Media (Secondary)**

Who: Impact investors, clean-tech VCs, construction-tech media, sustainability journalists. They're scanning LinkedIn for the next breakout company in the space.

What makes them stop scrolling: Traction signals. "5 homes built in 15 days." "Delivered to 3 countries." Client waitlist numbers. Revenue growth hints. The Katerra comparison (they all know that story). Factory automation roadmap.

What they don't care about: Product specs. They care about market size, defensibility, and growth trajectory.

## 5. VISUAL GUIDELINES ‚Äî What actually works

### Carousels (Priority #1)

**Dimensions:** 1080 √ó 1350px (4:5 vertical). This takes maximum feed real estate on mobile, which is where 57% of LinkedIn usage happens.

**Slide count:** 8-10. Under 8 doesn't build enough momentum. Over 12 loses people.

**Words per slide:** 25-50 max. If you're writing paragraphs on slides, you're making a document, not a carousel.

**Slide 1 (The Hook):** No logo. No intro. Just the hook. Big text. One sentence. This slide's only job is to make someone swipe. Example: "We tested straw vs. polystyrene at 35¬∞C." White or black background. Nothing else.

**Slides 2-8 (The Content):** One idea per slide. One data point per slide. Use contrast ‚Äî if slide 2 shows polystyrene failing, slide 3 shows straw succeeding. Visual rhythm: data slide ‚Üí insight slide ‚Üí data slide ‚Üí insight slide.

**Last Slide (The Close):** Not a CTA to your website. Not your logo. A provocative question or a single-sentence summary that makes people comment. "Would you live in a house made of straw?" works. "Visit biobuilds.com" doesn't.

**Design direction:** Clean. Not corporate-clean ‚Äî engineering-clean. Think Dieter Rams, not McKinsey. Dark backgrounds with white text for data. Real photography of your product on lifestyle slides. No stock photos ever. Your houses are beautiful. Use them.

**Typography:** One font. Sans-serif. Bold for headlines, regular for supporting text. No decorative fonts. No gradients. No drop shadows. You're selling precision ‚Äî the design should feel precise.

**Color palette:** Pull from your brand. But every carousel needs high contrast between background and text. If someone is scrolling at 7 AM on a bus in Munich, they need to read your slide in 0.5 seconds.

**Export:** PDF for upload to LinkedIn (this triggers the document/carousel format). Not images.

### Images

**What works:** Real photos from your factory, your builds, your clients' homes. Thermal camera images. Side-by-side comparisons. A wrapped module on a truck. A family standing in front of their just-delivered home. The FƒÉgƒÉra»ô mountain house drone shot.

**What doesn't work:** Renders (people are trained to distrust them). Stock photos. Over-designed infographics with 47 data points. Screenshots of your website.

**The single image rule:** If you're posting text + one image, that image needs to create a question. The wrapped white module with "YOUR PASSIVE HOME IS HERE" printed on it ‚Äî that creates a question. A pretty exterior shot of a finished house ‚Äî that doesn't. It's nice, but it doesn't stop a thumb.

### Video

**What works now:** Vertical (9:16 or 4:5). Under 90 seconds. Raw over polished. Subtitles mandatory (85% of LinkedIn video is watched on mute). Face-to-camera narration from Alin outperforms b-roll montages.

**What doesn't work:** Your 1-minute supercut videos with music and no narration. They look great. They generate 0 comments. The algorithm sees views but no conversation and deprioritizes them.

**The shift to make:** Instead of a polished 60-second edit of a house being assembled, try Alin holding a phone, standing on the site, saying "This family's house arrived 20 minutes ago. Right now it's on a truck behind me. By lunch, they'll have keys. Let me show you what's happening." That video, shaky and real, will outperform the cinematic version.

## 7. POSTING MECHANICS

**When:** Tuesday through Thursday. 8-10 AM CET. This hits the DACH morning commute plus EU professional browsing hours. Tuesday runs 20% higher engagement than Friday. Saturday and Sunday get 45% less engagement than weekday peaks.

Carousels perform best at 9-10:30 AM. Video at 8-9 AM. Text posts at 8-10 AM.

**Post length:** 800-1,200 characters for text posts. Below 5th-grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid). Short paragraphs. Line break every 2-3 lines. The "see more" fold on LinkedIn appears at ~210 characters on mobile ‚Äî your first 210 characters ARE the hook. Treat them like a headline.

**Hashtags:** Maximum 3 per post. LinkedIn removed hashtag following and actively penalizes 3+ tags (29% less reach measured across 1.8M posts). Use only:
- #PassiveHouse (your core identifier)
- #HealthyHomes (emotional/health angle)
- One rotating niche tag relevant to the specific post

Never: #FutureOfLiving #ImpactOverEgo #CarbonSmart #DesignForPeace ‚Äî these are vanity tags with zero discovery value. Kill them all.

**Links:** Never in the post body. Even though LinkedIn officially says there's no penalty, every independent dataset shows 25-60% less initial distribution for posts with links. If you absolutely need one, put it in the SECOND comment (not the first ‚Äî self-commenting now hurts reach).

**The Golden Hour:** The first 60-90 minutes after posting determine ~70% of a post's total lifetime reach. Comments carry 2x the weight of likes during this window. Alin should be actively responding to every comment during this period. Have the team engage within 15-20 minutes with real, substantive comments.

**Self-commenting:** Don't. This was a hack that worked in 2023-2024. Van der Blom's 2025 data shows it now limits reach. Let someone else be the first commenter.

**Carousel upload:** Export as PDF. Upload natively (don't use third-party scheduling tools for carousels ‚Äî some strip the document format and convert to image posts, which perform differently).
